# Boxing Footwork

Footwork is the engine of boxing. It controls distance, creates angles, generates power, and enables defensive movement. The best punchers in history were exceptional movers first.

## Fundamental Principles

### Stance Width
- Feet should be roughly shoulder-width apart at all times.
- If you step with one foot, the other must follow to maintain the stance width.
- This is the "step-drag" or "push-pull" principle.

### Step-Drag Movement

The basic rule: move the foot closest to the direction of travel first, then drag the other foot to restore stance width.

| Direction | Lead Foot | Trail Action |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Forward | Lead foot steps forward | Rear foot drags forward |
| Backward | Rear foot steps back | Lead foot drags back |
| Left (orthodox) | Lead foot steps left | Rear foot follows |
| Right (orthodox) | Rear foot steps right | Lead foot follows |

### Weight on Balls of Feet
- Stay on the balls of your feet at all times. Never flat-footed.
- The heels hover just above the floor (1-2 cm).
- This allows instant movement in any direction.

### Small Steps
- Take many small steps rather than few large ones.
- Small steps maintain balance; large steps create vulnerability.
- A typical step is 6-12 inches depending on the situation.

## Lateral Movement

### Moving Left (Orthodox)
1. Push off the ball of the rear foot.
2. Step the lead foot to the left (6-8 inches).
3. Immediately drag the rear foot to restore stance width.
4. You are now at a new angle relative to the opponent.

### Moving Right (Orthodox)
1. Push off the ball of the lead foot.
2. Step the rear foot to the right.
3. Drag the lead foot to follow.

### When to Move Laterally
- To avoid straight punches (lateral movement takes you off the centre line).
- To create new punching angles.
- To escape the ropes or corner.
- To circle an aggressive opponent.

## Forward and Backward Movement

### Advancing
- Step the lead foot forward, push off the rear foot.
- Drag the rear foot to maintain stance.
- Advance behind the jab -- never walk forward with hands down.

### Retreating
- Step the rear foot backward, push off the lead foot.
- Drag the lead foot to maintain stance.
- Retreat should be tactical, not panicked. Throw punches while moving back.

## Pivots

### Lead Foot Pivot
1. Plant the ball of the lead foot.
2. Rotate on the lead foot, swinging the rear foot in an arc.
3. You change your facing angle by 45-90 degrees while staying in place.

**Uses**: Escaping the corner, repositioning against a rushing opponent, creating angles for hooks.

### Rear Foot Pivot
1. Plant the ball of the rear foot.
2. Rotate on the rear foot, swinging the lead foot in an arc.
3. Less common but useful for specific counter situations.

## Cutting the Ring

When your opponent tries to move laterally, you need to cut them off rather than chase them.

### Principles
1. **Move to where they are going**, not where they are.
2. **Take diagonal steps** to close the angle.
3. **Use the jab** to slow their lateral movement.
4. **Herd them toward the ropes or corner** by consistently cutting off one escape route.

### Common Mistake
Chasing the opponent in a circle. This keeps the same distance and angle. Instead, take steps toward the centre of the ring while they circle, cutting their path shorter with each step.

## Angles and Positioning

### Creating Angles
- After throwing a combination, step to one side rather than staying directly in front.
- A 30-degree angle change after the 1-2 combination makes the opponent have to readjust before they can counter.
- Step to the left after the cross (for orthodox) to be outside the opponent's power hand.

### Ring Position
- Control the centre of the ring when possible. The fighter in the centre has more movement options.
- Avoid the ropes and corners -- they eliminate retreat and lateral options.
- If pushed to the ropes, pivot out rather than trying to walk straight back to the centre.

## Common Footwork Mistakes

1. **Crossing the feet**: Never let the feet cross during lateral movement. This creates a moment of zero balance.
2. **Bouncing too much**: Wastes energy and telegraphs movement. Stay smooth and grounded.
3. **Flat feet**: Limits reaction speed. Stay on the balls of the feet.
4. **Steps too large**: Big steps take longer and create balance vulnerabilities.
5. **Feet too close together**: Reduces the base of support and makes you easy to push off balance.
6. **Feet too far apart**: Slows movement and limits the ability to change direction quickly.
7. **Standing still after punching**: Always move after a combination. Staying stationary makes you a target.
8. **Not returning to stance**: Every step should end in a proper fighting stance.

## Footwork Drills

1. **Box Step Drill**: Imagine a square on the floor (2ft x 2ft). Step to each corner using step-drag movement. Forward, right, back, left. Then reverse. Do 3 rounds of 3 minutes.
2. **Cone Drill**: Place 4 cones in a diamond pattern. Move between cones using step-drag footwork while maintaining stance and throwing jabs.
3. **Mirror Drill**: Face a partner. One leads, the other mirrors their movement. Switch roles every 30 seconds.
4. **Ladder Drill**: Use an agility ladder. Move through it laterally and forward/backward while maintaining a boxing stance.
5. **Pivot Drill**: Stand at the heavy bag. Throw a 1-2, then pivot 90 degrees. Repeat around the bag.
6. **Shadow Box for Footwork**: Spend an entire round focused only on movement. No punches. Just movement with proper stance.
