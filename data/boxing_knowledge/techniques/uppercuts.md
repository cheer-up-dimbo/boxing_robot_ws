# Uppercuts: Left Uppercut and Right Uppercut

The uppercut is a vertical punch that travels upward from below the opponent's line of sight. It is devastatingly effective at close range, targeting the chin, solar plexus, or body. The upward trajectory makes it difficult to see coming when thrown correctly.

## Left Uppercut (Lead Uppercut for Orthodox)

### Proper Form

1. **Starting Position**: Fighting stance, weight balanced, hands at chin.
2. **Dip**: Bend the knees slightly and drop the lead shoulder a few inches. This loads the legs and creates the upward drive.
3. **Drive Upward**: Push off the lead foot, rotating the lead hip upward. The fist travels vertically from waist level to chin level.
4. **Fist Orientation**: Palm faces toward you throughout the punch. The knuckles point toward the ceiling at impact.
5. **Elbow Underneath**: The elbow stays close to the body and drives upward beneath the fist.
6. **Recovery**: Return the fist to the chin along the same path. Reset the knees and stance.

### Key Points
- The power comes from the legs and hip rotation, not from scooping with the arm.
- The dip should be subtle -- just enough to load the spring. Large dips telegraph the punch.
- Keep the rear hand high and tight against the chin throughout.

## Right Uppercut (Rear Uppercut for Orthodox)

### Proper Form

1. **Starting Position**: Fighting stance, slight bend in the knees.
2. **Dip**: Lower the rear shoulder and bend the knees. The rear elbow drops slightly toward the hip.
3. **Drive**: Explode upward from the legs, rotating the rear hip forward and upward. The fist rises from below the opponent's guard.
4. **Fist Path**: Straight upward, tight to the body. Palm faces inward.
5. **Full Extension**: The punch lands at chin height with the forearm roughly vertical.
6. **Recovery**: Pull back to guard immediately.

### Key Points
- The right uppercut is typically more powerful than the left because it uses the full kinetic chain from the rear side.
- It pairs exceptionally well after a left hook, which pulls the opponent's guard to one side and opens the chin for the uppercut.
- Avoid leaning backward -- stay centred over your base.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Scooping / looping the punch | Using arm instead of legs | Focus on driving straight up from the knees |
| Excessive dip | Over-loading before the punch | Keep the dip to 2-3 inches maximum |
| Leaning back | Trying to create distance for the upward path | Stay centred; the power is vertical, not backward |
| Elbow flaring wide | Poor arm path | Keep elbow tight to ribs as you drive upward |
| Thumb pointing up | Incorrect fist rotation | Palm faces you; knuckles toward ceiling |
| Dropping opposite hand | Counterbalancing the dip | Consciously press rear glove to cheekbone |
| Telegraphing with eyes | Looking at the body before punching up | Keep eyes on the opponent's chest/chin |

## Uppercut Variations

### Short Uppercut
Thrown at very close range with minimal wind-up. Almost purely a leg-and-hip-driven pop. Effective in the clinch or at infighting distance.

### Rear Uppercut to Body
Instead of targeting the chin, aim for the solar plexus or floating ribs. Bend the knees deeper and drive upward into the midsection. Extremely effective against opponents who lean forward.

### Lead Uppercut as Counter
When the opponent throws a straight punch, slip to the inside and drive the lead uppercut under their extended arm. High-level counter technique.

### Uppercut from Crouch
After ducking under a hook, rise with an uppercut using the upward momentum of your body. The entire body weight travels into the punch.

## Drills for Improving Uppercuts

1. **Heavy Bag Close Work (3 rounds)**: Stand within arm's length of the bag. Throw only uppercuts, alternating left and right. Focus on driving from the legs.
2. **Squat to Uppercut**: Perform a quarter squat, then explode upward throwing an uppercut at the peak. Develops the leg-drive connection.
3. **Slip-Uppercut Combo**: Slip a straight punch (from partner or double-end bag), then throw an uppercut on the rise. Builds the counter-uppercut reflex.
4. **1-2-5 Drill**: Jab-Cross-Left Uppercut. The jab and cross draw the guard forward, opening the chin for the uppercut underneath.
5. **Body-Head Uppercut**: Throw left uppercut to body, right uppercut to head. Trains level changing and combination flow.
6. **Medicine Ball Scoop**: Hold a light medicine ball and perform the uppercut motion, releasing at the top. Builds explosive upward power.

## When to Use Uppercuts

- At close range when the opponent's guard is high (punching underneath the guard)
- After slipping inside a straight punch
- As the finishing punch in a combination (e.g., 1-2-5-2)
- When the opponent dips their head low (meets the rising fist)
- To the body against an opponent pressing forward
- Rising out of a crouch or duck under a hook
