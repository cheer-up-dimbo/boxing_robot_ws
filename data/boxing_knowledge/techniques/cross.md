# The Cross (Straight Right / Straight Left)

The cross is the primary power punch in boxing. For an orthodox fighter it is the straight right hand; for a southpaw it is the straight left. It travels in a straight line from the rear hand to the target, generating power through hip rotation and weight transfer.

## Proper Form

1. **Starting Position**: Begin in fighting stance, weight balanced, hands at chin level.
2. **Hip Rotation**: Drive the rear hip forward, pivoting on the ball of the rear foot. The hip leads the hand.
3. **Shoulder Extension**: The rear shoulder pushes forward as the fist travels straight toward the target. The lead shoulder pulls back naturally.
4. **Fist Path**: The fist travels from chin level in a straight line. The palm rotates to face down at full extension.
5. **Full Extension**: At impact, the rear shoulder protects the chin on that side. The lead hand stays up guarding the opposite side.
6. **Retraction**: Pull the hand straight back to the chin. Reset the hip and foot position.

## Key Mechanics

- **Weight Transfer**: Weight shifts from the rear foot (60/40) to a more centred position during the punch. Do not overcommit forward.
- **Kinetic Chain**: Power flows from floor, through legs, through hips, through torso, through shoulder, and out through the fist. Breaking the chain at any point reduces power.
- **Rear Foot Pivot**: The ball of the rear foot pivots inward as the hip rotates. The heel lifts off the ground.
- **Alignment**: Fist, wrist, and forearm must form a straight line at impact to prevent wrist injury.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Winding up (pulling hand back first) | Trying to generate more power | Power comes from hips, not arm pullback |
| Looping the punch | Poor straight-line mechanics | Practice on the double-end bag for accuracy |
| Dropping the lead hand | Counterbalancing | Consciously keep lead hand on chin |
| Overextending / lunging | Opponent is out of range | Step with the jab first to close distance |
| No hip rotation | Arm-punching | Drill hip pivots without punching, then add the hand |
| Rising up on punch | Trying to reach higher | Bend knees slightly, maintain level |

## Cross Variations

### Power Cross
Full commitment: larger hip turn, deeper weight transfer. Used when you have a clear opening.

### Counter Cross
Thrown simultaneously as the opponent jabs. Slip outside the jab and drive the cross over the top. High-level timing skill.

### Cross to the Body
Lower the level by bending the knees (not bending at the waist). Drive the cross into the opponent's midsection.

### Check Hook into Cross
After a lead check hook shifts the opponent's guard, immediately follow with the cross to the newly exposed side.

## Drills for Improving the Cross

1. **Heavy Bag Power Rounds (3 rounds)**: Focus exclusively on the cross. Emphasise hip rotation on every repetition. Feel the floor connection.
2. **1-2 Drill (3 rounds)**: Throw jab-cross combinations continuously. The jab sets the range, the cross delivers the power.
3. **Pivot and Cross**: Step offline at a 45-degree angle and throw the cross. Develops the ability to punch from angles.
4. **Medicine Ball Rotation**: Hold a medicine ball at chest height and rotate explosively. Mimics the torso mechanics of the cross.
5. **Partner Mitt Work**: Partner holds focus mitts at head and body level, calls for the cross at various heights and angles.

## When to Use the Cross

- As the second punch in the 1-2 (jab-cross) combination
- As a counter after slipping or parrying the opponent's jab
- Following a hook to the body that drops the opponent's guard on the opposite side
- To finish a combination with authority
- Against an opponent moving forward (using their momentum against them)

## BoxBunny Detection Notes

The cross is identified by the CV model through the rear-hand extension and significant hip rotation. On the BoxBunny system it typically registers on the **centre** or **right** pad. The cross has a longer travel time than the jab, so the IMU fusion window comfortably captures both the CV detection and pad impact.
