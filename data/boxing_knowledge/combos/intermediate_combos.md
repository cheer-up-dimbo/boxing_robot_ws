# Intermediate Combinations

These combinations build on the beginner set by adding uppercuts, body-head level changes, and more complex flow patterns. Fighters should be comfortable with all 10 beginner combos before moving to these.

## Prerequisites

- Solid jab, cross, hook, and uppercut technique
- Ability to throw 4+ punch combinations without losing balance
- Basic understanding of defensive head movement between punches

## The 10 Intermediate Combinations

### Combo 1: Jab-Cross-Lead Uppercut (1-2-5)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Lead Uppercut
**Purpose**: The jab and cross pull the guard forward and high; the lead uppercut attacks underneath.
**Focus**: After the cross, dip the knees slightly and drive the uppercut from below. The transition should feel like the cross pulls you into the uppercut.
**Target**: Chin or solar plexus.

### Combo 2: Jab-Lead Uppercut-Cross (1-5-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Lead Uppercut -- Cross
**Purpose**: The uppercut between the jab and cross is unexpected. It disrupts the guard vertically before the cross comes over the top.
**Focus**: The jab measures distance, the uppercut lifts the opponent's chin, and the cross capitalises on the exposed target.

### Combo 3: Jab-Cross-Hook-Rear Uppercut (1-2-3-6)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Lead Hook -- Rear Uppercut
**Purpose**: A four-punch combination that attacks from every angle: straight, lateral, and vertical.
**Focus**: The hook turns the opponent's head to one side; the rear uppercut comes up on the other side while they are recovering.

### Combo 4: Double Jab-Cross-Lead Hook-Cross (1-1-2-3-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Jab -- Cross -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: Five-punch high-volume combination. Overwhelms the opponent with sustained pressure.
**Focus**: Maintain balance throughout all five punches. The key is consistent rhythm without pausing.

### Combo 5: Jab to Body-Cross to Head (1b-2)
**Sequence**: Jab to body -- Cross to head
**Purpose**: The body jab drops the opponent's guard low, exposing the head for the cross.
**Focus**: Dip the knees for the body jab, then rise into the cross. The level change makes this highly effective.

### Combo 6: Lead Hook to Body-Lead Hook to Head (3b-3)
**Sequence**: Lead Hook to body -- Lead Hook to head
**Purpose**: The double hook at two levels. The body hook draws the elbow down; the head hook exploits the opening.
**Focus**: The body hook and head hook use the same rotation. The only difference is the height of the fist. Keep the rhythm tight -- body hook, head hook, no pause.

### Combo 7: Jab-Cross-Lead Hook to Body-Lead Uppercut (1-2-3b-5)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Lead Hook to body -- Lead Uppercut
**Purpose**: Attacks head, head, body, then rises from the body to the chin with the uppercut.
**Focus**: The body hook loads the legs for the uppercut. Use the dip from the body hook to spring into the uppercut.

### Combo 8: Rear Uppercut-Lead Hook-Cross (6-3-2)
**Sequence**: Rear Uppercut -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: A power combination that starts low and finishes high. Effective in close range.
**Focus**: The uppercut lifts the opponent's chin; the hook catches the side of the head; the cross finishes straight down the middle.

### Combo 9: Jab-Cross-Slip-Cross (1-2-Slip-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Slip (the anticipated counter) -- Cross
**Purpose**: Introduces defensive movement within the combination. The slip avoids the opponent's counter and the second cross punishes them.
**Focus**: The slip must be quick and shallow. You are not evading a specific punch -- you are building the habit of defensive movement between bursts.

### Combo 10: Lead Hook-Rear Uppercut-Lead Hook (3-6-3)
**Sequence**: Lead Hook -- Rear Uppercut -- Lead Hook
**Purpose**: Three-punch close-range combination that attacks from alternating angles.
**Focus**: The first hook turns the guard, the uppercut attacks underneath, and the second hook catches the now-open side. Maintain balance on the balls of the feet throughout.

## Training Approach

1. **Drill each combo 20 times in shadow boxing** before moving to the bag.
2. **Add footwork**: Step forward into the combo, then step out after the last punch.
3. **Add defense**: After each combo, add a slip or step-back to simulate realistic exchanges.
4. **Mix combos**: Randomly alternate between beginner and intermediate combos in a round.
5. **Timer work**: Set a timer for 10-second intervals. Throw one combo per interval with active footwork between.

## Key Differences from Beginner Level

- Combinations are longer (4-5 punches)
- Level changes (body to head and head to body) are integrated
- Uppercuts are incorporated alongside jabs, crosses, and hooks
- Defensive movement (slips) appears within combinations
- Close-range and mid-range combinations are both included
