# Beginner Combinations

Boxing combinations use a numbering system that is standard across most gyms. Understanding this system is essential for following drill instructions and communicating with coaches.

## Punch Number Key

| Number | Punch |
|--------|-------|
| 1 | Jab |
| 2 | Cross (straight right for orthodox) |
| 3 | Lead hook (left hook for orthodox) |
| 4 | Rear hook (right hook for orthodox) |
| 5 | Lead uppercut (left uppercut for orthodox) |
| 6 | Rear uppercut (right uppercut for orthodox) |
| b | Body (modifier -- e.g., 1b = jab to body) |

## The 10 Beginner Combinations

### Combo 1: The Jab (1)
**Sequence**: Jab
**Purpose**: The single jab is a combination unto itself. It is the most thrown punch in boxing.
**Focus**: Speed, accuracy, and a fast return to guard. Practise throwing 2-3 jabs in a row at varying speeds.

### Combo 2: Jab-Cross (1-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross
**Purpose**: The bread-and-butter combination. The jab measures distance and distracts; the cross delivers power.
**Focus**: The jab and cross should flow as one motion. The hip rotation for the cross begins as the jab retracts.
**Timing**: The cross should land within 0.2-0.3 seconds after the jab.

### Combo 3: Double Jab-Cross (1-1-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Jab -- Cross
**Purpose**: The double jab disrupts the opponent's timing more than a single jab. The second jab often lands because the opponent relaxes after the first.
**Focus**: Vary the speed of the two jabs. Throw the first light and the second sharp, then follow with the cross.

### Combo 4: Jab-Cross-Hook (1-2-3)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Lead Hook
**Purpose**: This is the classic three-punch combination. The jab and cross move the opponent's guard to the centre; the hook attacks from the side.
**Focus**: The hook should flow naturally from the cross. As the cross retracts, the body is already rotating for the hook.

### Combo 5: Jab-Cross-Hook-Cross (1-2-3-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: Extends the 1-2-3 with a finishing cross. The second cross catches the opponent recovering from the hook.
**Focus**: Maintain balance throughout all four punches. The second cross should be just as sharp as the first.

### Combo 6: Jab-Body Cross (1-2b)
**Sequence**: Jab to head -- Cross to body
**Purpose**: Level change. The jab draws the guard up; the cross attacks the exposed midsection.
**Focus**: Bend the knees to reach the body -- do not bend at the waist. The cross to the body should be just as mechanically sound as the head version.

### Combo 7: Jab-Jab (1-1)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Jab
**Purpose**: Volume and distance control. Double jabs keep the opponent on the defensive and score points.
**Focus**: Both jabs should snap back completely before the next one goes out. Do not let the arm float between jabs.

### Combo 8: Jab-Cross-Body Hook (1-2-3b)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Lead Hook to body
**Purpose**: Attacks head level then drops to the body. The liver shot (left hook to body against orthodox opponents) is one of the most effective punches in boxing.
**Focus**: After the cross, dip the knees and rotate into the body hook. Keep the rear hand high.

### Combo 9: Cross-Hook-Cross (2-3-2)
**Sequence**: Cross -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: A power combination that starts with the rear hand. Best used as a counter or when the opening is already created.
**Focus**: The first cross must be committed to set up the hook. The rhythm should be even: bang-bang-bang.

### Combo 10: Jab-Cross-Body Cross (1-2-2b)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross to head -- Cross to body
**Purpose**: The head-to-body cross is unexpected because most opponents expect a different punch after the second.
**Focus**: The body cross follows the same path as the head cross but angles downward. Bend the knees for the body shot.

## Training Tips for Beginners

- **Start slow**: Learn each combination at half speed before adding power.
- **Shadow box first**: Throw the combo in the air 20 times before hitting the bag.
- **3-round structure**: Round 1 at 50% speed, Round 2 at 75% speed, Round 3 at full speed.
- **Always return to guard**: Every combination ends with both hands returning to chin level.
- **Breathe**: Exhale sharply with each punch. Holding your breath causes early fatigue.
- **Footwork**: Step into the combination. Do not throw while standing flat-footed and stationary.
