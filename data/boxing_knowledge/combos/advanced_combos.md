# Advanced Combinations

Advanced combinations incorporate feints, defensive movement within the sequence, angle changes, and complex level shifting. These combos require excellent balance, timing, and the ability to read and adapt mid-combination.

## Prerequisites

- Mastery of all beginner and intermediate combinations
- Clean uppercut technique from both hands
- Ability to slip and pivot without losing balance
- Experience with body-head level changes
- Comfortable throwing 5+ punch combinations

## The 10 Advanced Combinations

### Combo 1: Jab-Feint Cross-Lead Hook-Cross (1-Feint 2-3-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Feint the cross (shoulder roll without committing the hand) -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: The feinted cross pulls the opponent's guard to the centre, exposing the side of the head for the hook. The real cross follows once the guard has shifted.
**Focus**: The feint must be convincing. Rotate the hip and shoulder as if throwing the cross, but keep the fist at chin level. The hook launches from the feint position.

### Combo 2: Jab-Cross-Pivot Left-Lead Hook-Cross (1-2-Pivot-3-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Pivot 45 degrees left -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: The pivot changes your angle after the initial 1-2, making the hook land from a direction the opponent is not defending.
**Focus**: The pivot should be one smooth step, not a stop-and-restart. The hook fires as the pivot completes.

### Combo 3: Lead Uppercut-Rear Uppercut-Lead Hook-Cross (5-6-3-2)
**Sequence**: Lead Uppercut -- Rear Uppercut -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: An inside fighting combination that starts low and works to the head. Devastating at close range.
**Focus**: The double uppercut lifts the opponent's chin and guard; the hook and cross follow while they are off balance. Stay compact throughout -- no wide swings.

### Combo 4: Jab to Body-Rear Uppercut-Lead Hook-Cross (1b-6-3-2)
**Sequence**: Jab to body -- Rear Uppercut -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: The body jab forces the opponent to lower their guard, the uppercut exploits the opening, and the hook-cross finishes the sequence.
**Focus**: The level change from body jab to uppercut should be fluid. The knees do the work of changing levels, not the waist.

### Combo 5: Jab-Cross-Slip Right-Lead Hook to Body-Lead Uppercut-Cross (1-2-Slip-3b-5-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Cross -- Slip to the right -- Lead Hook to body -- Lead Uppercut -- Cross
**Purpose**: A six-movement sequence that integrates offense, defense, and level changes. The slip avoids the anticipated counter, then the attack resumes at body level and works upward.
**Focus**: Rhythm and flow. Each movement sets up the next. The slip loads the body for the hook; the body hook loads for the uppercut; the uppercut opens the chin for the cross.

### Combo 6: Lead Hook-Cross-Lead Hook to Body-Rear Uppercut (3-2-3b-6)
**Sequence**: Lead Hook to head -- Cross -- Lead Hook to body -- Rear Uppercut
**Purpose**: Attacks head-head-body-head, with each level change forcing the opponent to readjust their guard.
**Focus**: The first hook and cross keep the guard high. The body hook drops underneath, and the rear uppercut rises from the low position with maximum leg drive.

### Combo 7: Double Jab-Feint Cross-Lead Uppercut-Cross-Lead Hook (1-1-Feint 2-5-2-3)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Jab -- Feint Cross -- Lead Uppercut -- Cross -- Lead Hook
**Purpose**: The double jab and feinted cross are three actions that manipulate the opponent's guard before the real attack begins with the uppercut.
**Focus**: The first three movements should feel like a setup phase. The last three are the payload. Patience in the setup creates the opening.

### Combo 8: Rear Hook-Lead Uppercut-Rear Hand to Body-Lead Hook (4-5-2b-3)
**Sequence**: Rear Hook -- Lead Uppercut -- Cross to body -- Lead Hook
**Purpose**: A rare-starting combination that catches the opponent off guard. The rear hook is uncommonly used as an opener, creating surprise.
**Focus**: The rear hook requires full hip commitment. The following three punches flow naturally from the momentum of the initial rotation.

### Combo 9: Jab-Slip Left-Cross-Lead Hook-Step Right-Cross (1-Slip-2-3-Step-2)
**Sequence**: Jab -- Slip Left -- Cross -- Lead Hook -- Step Right -- Cross
**Purpose**: Integrates two defensive movements (slip and step) with four punches. Simulates a realistic exchange where you punch, avoid, punch, reposition, and punch again.
**Focus**: Each defensive movement creates a new angle. The cross after the slip comes from outside the opponent's guard; the cross after the step comes from the opposite side.

### Combo 10: Lead Hook-Rear Hook-Lead Uppercut-Rear Uppercut-Lead Hook-Cross (3-4-5-6-3-2)
**Sequence**: Lead Hook -- Rear Hook -- Lead Uppercut -- Rear Uppercut -- Lead Hook -- Cross
**Purpose**: The ultimate six-punch combination. Alternating hooks and uppercuts create a storm of angles, finishing with the clean 3-2. Requires exceptional balance and cardio.
**Focus**: Stay compact. Every punch is short and tight. The power comes from rapid hip alternation. If any punch gets sloppy, the entire combination falls apart. Drill at slow speed for 100 repetitions before attempting at full speed.

## Training Approach

1. **Decompose**: Break each combo into pairs. Master the first two movements, then add the third, and so on.
2. **Shadow box at 40% speed**: Spend a full round on a single advanced combo at slow speed.
3. **Mirror work**: Watch your balance and guard maintenance during the longer sequences.
4. **Partner drill**: Have a partner hold mitts and call adjustments mid-combo.
5. **Pressure test**: Try the combo while your partner applies light forward pressure.
